Gearbox of a Fan

Disclaimer: The number of teeth on each gear, the number of teeth on each gear, and the overall design of the box is a blown up version of a toy fan I obtained from a 99 cents store. The design is not my own. I only wanted to model and print a version of it.

Status: Work in progress

Notes: Most of the time was spent researching how to model crown gears and how to model spur gears that meshed with crown gears. Before I continue with this project, I'd like to print these pieces out to make sure they work together. I'll have access to a 3D printer once I enter college, but before then I'll have to wait.

Next Steps:

  1. Print out the parts and assemble them
  2. Model a fan to attach to the gear box
  3. Print out the fan and test to see if it attaches to the gearbox properly.
  4. Optionally: Create a case around the gearbox so it looks like a fan.

Waterwheel

Status: Finished

Notes: Although this project is finished, I would like to rework the fountain again if I have time.

  • Because I relied on hot glue gun to attach the pieces together, the fountain was not extremely sturdy; it broke after being knocked around in a tote bag. I'd like to attempt some sort of supports at the end of each printed part to make the connections sturdier.
  • The elevated basin also was not effective in directing the flow of water where I wanted it to go, hence the addition of a 3D printed plate I had lying around on the top of the elevated basin.
  • The spout on the elevated basin did not print properly and I'd like to have been able to print another to see if it was a fault in the design.
  • The cut in the lower basin made for the wire was either unneccessary or the pieces should have been glued so that the wire could rest on the cut easily.
  • The printed materials were unfortunately of different colors because of limited supplies of resin in one color.
  • The direction of the waterwheel should be switched so that the paddles actually catch the water.

Final Product

  • A wheel bearing is inserted inside the waterwheel as suggested by a classmate in order to make the rotation of the wheel smooth.
  • A transparent tube is inserted into the middle of the vertical poles to pump water from the lower basin to the upper basin.

Original Model

  • Because the circle disks and rings turned out to be unnecessary, they were not printed due to time contraints.
  • In hindsight, the slot in the lower basin should have been shifted closer to the base of the support for the upper basin.
